Modes for Outward Remittance

Wire Transfer/ Telegraphic Transfer (TT)

Wire Transfer is an electronic mode of fund transfer through the SWIFT network with which you can transfer money from your Indian Bank account to an overseas bank account. Indian Banks and RBI approved Authorized Dealers have arrangements with over 400+ major banks across the globe to send and receive SWIFT messages which ensures safe and secure outward remittance from India.

Foreign Currency Demand Draft (DD)-

A Foreign Currency DD is another facility for making an international money transfer for VISA fees or migrating to other countries etc. DDs are made 'favouring' the beneficiary and are either mailed by the bank which created them to the beneficiary bank abroad or can be personally deposited in the bank abroad. Credit of funds to the beneficiary account happens only after the realization of the DD at the beneficiary bank and this could take anywhere between 2 to 12 days depending on how the DD is being sent abroad.
